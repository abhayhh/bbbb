The Scenic Route: A Coastal Bus Journey Through Italy

By Jane Doe · July 25, 2024

There’s a certain magic to discovering a country from the window of a bus. While trains offer speed and planes offer distance, the humble bus provides an unfiltered, ground-level view of the world passing by. Our recent trip along the Italian coast was a testament to this, a journey filled with winding roads, breathtaking vistas, and the charming chaos of local life.

The Journey Begins: From Rome to the Amalfi Coast

Our adventure started in the eternal city, but the real story began once we left the bustling metropolis behind. The bus ride towards the Amalfi Coast is not for the faint of heart, but the rewards are immeasurable. The road clings to the cliffs, revealing one spectacular view after another.

Tips for the Amalfi Bus Ride:

  • Sit on the right side: For the best coastal views when traveling south, grab a seat on the right side of the bus.
  • Travel light: Luggage space can be limited, so pack efficiently.
  • Embrace the curves: If you're prone to motion sickness, take precautions. The views are worth it!
"The world is a book, and those who do not travel read only one page." – Saint Augustine

This quote resonated deeply as we passed through small, unnamed villages, each with its own unique character. These are the places you miss when you're flying 30,000 feet above. We stopped in Positano, its colorful houses cascading down to the sea, a sight that no photograph can truly capture.
